Project Context: Uncertainty-Aware Attention CNN for Diabetic Retinopathy GradingHigh-Level Research OverviewDeveloping an uncertainty-aware deep learning system for diabetic retinopathy (DR) grading combining Attention mechanisms (CBAM) with Monte Carlo Dropout (Bayesian Approximation). The research focuses on "Safety AI," creating a model capable of quantifying its own doubt when diagnosing out-of-distribution cases like Messidor-2, bridging the gap between accuracy and clinical trust.+1Core ObjectivesArchitecture: Building a ResNet-50 backbone integrated with CBAM attention modules to focus on lesion-specific features.Uncertainty Quantification: Implementing Monte Carlo Dropout to estimate predictive (epistemic) uncertainty.Training Strategy: Training exclusively on the imbalanced APTOS 2019 dataset using Focal Loss and aggressive augmentation.Safety Evaluation: Validating on the external Messidor-2 dataset to analyze the correlation between "Model Uncertainty" and "Prediction Error".Clinical Relevance: Evaluating performance on 5-class grading and Binary Referable DR (Refer vs. Non-refer).The PipelinePhase 1: Advanced Preprocessing & EDABen Graham's Preprocessing: Uses a circular crop to remove black borders and local color normalization to mitigate lighting variations.Target Resolution: 512x512 is preferred, with 224x224 as a minimal baseline.Data Handling: Implements a stratified 80/20 split preserving class distribution.Augmentation: Uses geometric transformations like $\pm360^{\circ}$ rotation and flips, alongside regularization techniques like MixUp ($\alpha=0.2$) or CutMix.Phase 2: Model Architecture ImplementationBackbone: ImageNet Pretrained ResNet-50.Attention Module (The "Focus"): CBAM is inserted after each ResNet Stage (Layers 1-4) to enhance the representation of small lesions like microaneurysms.Bayesian Head (The "Doubt"): A Dropout layer ($p=0.5$) is inserted before the final Fully Connected layer and remains critically active during Inference/Testing.Phase 3: TrainingLoss Function: Focal Loss utilizing $\gamma=2.0$ and $\alpha$ weights balanced by inverse class frequency.Optimizer: AdamW with a learning rate of 1e-4 and a Cosine Annealing Scheduler.Monitor: Tracking Validation QWK and Validation Loss.Phase 4: Uncertainty-Aware Evaluation (The Core Innovation)Monte Carlo Sampling: Running $T=20$ forward passes with random dropout for each Messidor-2 image.Prediction: Calculating the mean of 20 probability vectors ($P_{mean}$).Uncertainty Score: Deriving the entropy of the mean distribution ($H = -\sum p \log p$) or Variance.Decision Making: Using Argmax of $P_{mean}$ for Grading, and referring patients if the Grade is $\ge 2$.Phase 5: Safety Analysis & VisualizationQuantitative Safety: Plotting Error vs. Uncertainty to check if wrong predictions have higher uncertainty, targeting a Pearson correlation $r>0.5$.Uncertainty Thresholding: Evaluating accuracy improvement by rejecting the top 10-20% most uncertain cases to "Refer to Human".Qualitative Explainability: Generating Grad-CAM maps to compare True Positives against False Positives and showing how Attention Maps become "messy" when uncertainty is high.MetricsPrimary: Quadratic Weighted Kappa (QWK) for the 5-class grading task.Clinical: AUC-ROC and Sensitivity/Specificity for Referable DR.Safety: Negative Log Likelihood (NLL) and Expected Calibration Error (ECE), which are optional but recommended.Implementation Timeline & Side NotesWeek 1-2: Setup Data Pipeline, Preprocessing, Vanilla ResNet Baseline, and implement CBAM for Attention-ResNet.Week 3-4: MC Dropout Inference on Messidor-2, analyze Uncertainty-Error correlation, and visualize Attention maps.Baseline: Always compare the Uncertainty Attention ResNet with a Standard ResNet to prove modifications help.Resolution: Avoid 224x224 since DR lesions are tiny; trade-off with 256x256 or 384x384 if 512x512 is too slow.Expectation: Accuracy on Messidor-2 might drop due to domain shift, but the Uncertainty Score must correlate with that drop as the success criteria.
